The Basics

Creating a Foundation

If you are reading this page, it is safe to assume that you want to improve your game. Below are the basic steps that you need to take to develop a good foundation:

Stance

Though it may not seem like much, your stance has an effect on your aim. You should always stand with your feet apart and one foot forward. Stay down for the entirety of your shot before you move and you should see more balls find their way to the pockets.

Stroke

Your stroke plays a huge role in your accuracy. If your storke isn't straight when you take your shot, then the ball will not roll straight. An easy exercise for this is to take a bottle with an opening not much larger than the diameter of your cue tip and practice stroking in and out of the bottle openeing. This can even be done without a pool table!

Aim

Knowing where to aim is vital. If you aren't aiming correctly, you will miss most of the shots that you take. One way to help improve your aim is to utilize something called a "ghost ball". Look at the ball you are wanting to make and the pocket you are aiming at. Find the point on the ball where you have to hit it to make it go to that pocket. Now, imagine where that cue ball has to hit. That is your "ghost ball".

Attitude

As with anything else you do in life, your attitude will always have an effect on what you do. Stay positive, clear minded and open to coachings and suggestions. If you approach the table frustrated, you are most likely not going to take your time or think a shot through. The best thing you can do is have fun and your skills will develop over time.

Once you have perfected those four basics, you are on your way to truly improving your game. Then you can begin working on safeties, cue ball control, english and so much more. This is one game where you will never stop learning and no two people see the table the same or shoot the same.
